# Interview guide — the question bank you draft before each session

**Status:** READY (worked instance — adapt per interviewee)
**Loaded when:** BEFORE any interview, as the first beat of the interview-assist loop.

This is the instrument you, the assistant, draft and hand the human interviewer before they walk in.
Its whole job is to defeat the official-version reflex: questions are phrased so the person retells
one concrete episode, not the sanctioned process. You build it from the brief and from prior
snapshots, you seed it with the branch's own workaround and exception probes, and after the interview
you diff what it asked against what the transcript actually surfaced and resharpen it for the next
person — deleting prompts that came back thin.

Draft one guide per interviewee. Keep it short enough to glance at mid-conversation.

---

## 1. Focus statement (one line)

What this specific interview most needs to surface, given who they are and what's still unknown.
Example: *"Recover how Maria actually closes a disputed case end to end, especially the steps the
case-management system doesn't know about."*

## 2. Concrete-instance prompts

Each opens a real episode, never a generality. Phrase them as "show me / walk me through the last
time," not "how do you usually."

- "Walk me through the last case you closed this morning — open the actual record, don't summarize."
- "When did that last go wrong? Take me through exactly what you did, step by step."
- "Show me the one from this week that was a pain. What made it different?"
- *(add 3–6 tuned to this role and what prior interviews left unresolved)*

## 3. Abstraction watchlist — interrupt on these

When the person reaches for a summary word, that is the cue to stop and ask for the specific episode.
Watch for: **"usually," "normally," "we always," "typically," "in theory," "the process is."** The
interrupt is always the same: *"When did that last actually happen? Walk me through that one."*

## 4. Workaround / exception probe tail

The happy path is easy and everyone agrees on it; the value and the risk live in the exceptions. End
every guide with probes that push past it:

- "What happens when it doesn't go the normal way?"
- "When the system won't let you do something, what do you do instead?"
- "Who do you email or call when you're stuck? What for?"
- "What do you keep in a spreadsheet (or your head) because the tool can't hold it?"
- "What's the weird case everyone forgets about until it shows up?"

## 5. Branch-specific probes

Paste in the probe set from whichever branch this interview serves (the workaround register, the
role-interest map, the glossary, and so on). Each branch README carries its own.

---

**After the interview:** diff this guide against the snapshot. Which prompts opened real episodes?
Which produced only summaries? Cut the dead ones, sharpen the live ones, and carry forward the
follow-ups the snapshot flagged into the next person's guide.
