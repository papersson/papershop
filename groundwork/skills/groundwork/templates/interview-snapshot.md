# Interview snapshot — one fixed-slot record per interview

**Status:** READY (worked instance — one per interview)
**Loaded when:** DURING and right AFTER an interview, as the second and third beats of the loop.

This is the single artifact a solo human interviewer most reliably fails to produce alone: a raw
conversation turned into a fixed-slot, comparable record. You structure the human's typed, dictated,
or recorded notes into these slots so they can stay present in the room; you do not promise to
structure live during sustained eye contact. Right after, you draft the finished snapshot and surface
the verbatim line and any newly named workaround for the human to **confirm before it enters
synthesis**.

Fill the same slots every time — sameness is what makes snapshots diffable across interviews.

---

## Snapshot: [name / role] — [date]

**Role & context:** who they are, which team, where they sit in the process, how long they've done it.

**The story, in steps:** the one concrete episode they walked through, as an ordered list of what they
actually did — including the steps the official process omits.
1.
2.
3.

**Pains & unmet needs:** where the current process fought them, slowed them, or made them improvise.

**Workarounds (ops slot):** each workaround they named, and the system or person it routes around.
This slot feeds the workaround register directly — never leave it empty without confirming there
genuinely were none.
- *When [trigger] → they [workaround] → routes around [system / person].*

**One verbatim quote:** a single exact sentence in their words, captured not paraphrased. The line
that most sharply names a pain or a workaround.

**Surprises & follow-ups:** anything that contradicted an earlier interview or your assumptions, and
the questions to carry into the next person's guide.

**Confidence caveat:** if this account was from memory rather than watched in situ, say so here in
plain words — "recounted, not observed; the forgotten-steps risk is high for the exception path." Do
not attach a false-precision number; a written caveat travels with the snapshot and lowers how hard
anything downstream leans on it.

---

**Before it enters synthesis:** read the verbatim quote and the workaround slot back to the human (or
to the interviewee if still in the room) to confirm you captured them right, not as you expected them.
